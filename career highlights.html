Iula Carter has had an illustrious career in education and intellectual property advocacy. With a background as a teachers' aide in the Dayton Public School System for two decades, she went on to establish and lead the Dayton/Miami Valley Educational Affiliate of the National Intellectual Property Law Association in 1991. A nonprofit organization focuses on supporting minority inventors through development, education, and training. Their efforts include disseminating information on patents, copyrights, and trademarks to minority groups.

Iula played a key role in establishing the library at Phillips Temple CME Church and was a founding member of Reed Chapel CME Church in Dayton, Ohio. Additionally, she was an active member of the Eastern Star Deborah Chapter for half a century. Her dedication to these institutions exemplifies her commitment to education and community service.

In recognition of her accomplishments, Iula Carter has been honored as one of the Dayton Daily News Top Ten Women and has been immortalized on the Dayton Walk of Fame. She is also an accomplished author, holding copyrights on three books: "Walking with God as My Shepherd," "I Want to Be an Inventor," and "Iula Wright Carter, A Lady of Faith and Determination."

Furthermore, Iula Carter's contributions have been documented in various publications, including "Dayton African American Heritage" by Margaret E. Petters, "Dayton Inventors: Facts and Fiction" by Curt Dalton, and "Innovative Spirit of African Americans" by Patricia Carter Sluby. Through her work and advocacy, Iula Carter has made a lasting impact on both the educational and intellectual property landscapes.
